PROTOTYPE: THE PACK
The Structural Anatomy

• The Chassis: The core is a heavy-duty Arborist’s Harness—thick, double-stitched nylon webbing that wraps around Slick’s thighs, waist, and chest. It’s reinforced with a "back-plate" of scavenged T6 aluminum, which serves as the mounting point for the engine.

• The Engine Block: A singular, central MHD Plasma Manifold sits between his shoulder blades. It’s exposed, revealing a tangled nest of copper induction coils and hand-soldered ceramic insulators. There is no sleek housing; when it’s idling, you can see the violet light of the plasma flickering through the gaps in the hardware.

• The "Veins": Heavy-gauge, orange high-voltage cables snake out from the back-plate, zip-tied to the nylon straps, and run down his arms to the hand-controls.

The Control Interface: "The Dead-Man Grips"

Instead of a neural link or a sophisticated HUD, the V1 uses Physical Feedback:

• The Palm Sliders: Slick holds two ergonomic handles that look like they were stripped from a high-end mountain bike. Sliding his thumbs forward increases the plasma flow (thrust); pulling his triggers activates the "Micro-Burst" for rapid maneuvers.

• Haptic Tension: The harness is designed to pull tight against his skeleton as thrust increases. It "bites" into his shoulders and hips, giving him a raw, physical sense of how much power he’s pushing. If the harness goes slack, he’s falling.

The Visual Aesthetic: "Industrial Raw"

• The Silhouette: In this rig, Slick looks thin and vulnerable. He’s just a man in a jumpsuit strapped to a machine. There is no "V-taper" or heroic bulk yet—just the jagged, asymmetrical outline of the engine on his back.

• The Heat Shielding: A crude sheet of aluminized mylar is tucked between the engine and his back to prevent his clothes from catching fire. It crinkles loudly every time he moves, making him sound more like a walking emergency blanket than a master villain.

• The "Ozone Stench": Because the ionization isn't perfectly contained, the harness is always surrounded by the sharp, metallic smell of ozone and the faint hiss of static electricity.

The Performance Profile: "The Vertical Jumper"

• Verticality vs. Flight: It’s excellent at "the jump." He can zip from the floor of a cavern to a ledge 80 feet up in two seconds.

• The Stability Problem: Because it lacks the Inverness flight surfaces, it has zero aerodynamic lift. He stays in the air purely through "brute force" thrust. If the engine cuts out for even a millisecond, he drops like a stone

TECHNICAL SPECIFICATION: THE RIG

Status: Operational (v.4.2) | User: Mic (Slick the Sly) | Paradigm: Manufacturing Savant

I. THE CHASSIS: KINETIC ENDOSKELETON

A low-profile, "sub-dermal" frame worn beneath civilian clothing.

• Materials: Topological-optimized titanium-alloy lattice and carbon-fiber spars.

• Structural Spine: A central load-bearing pillar anchored from the base of the skull to the coccyx. It distributes flight thrust and impact force across the entire skeletal structure to prevent "ragdoll" flailing.

• Impact Dampening: Utilizes Magnetorheological (MR) Fluid in joints and D3O Dilatant Fluid in chest/limb plates. These materials transition from flexible to rigid instantly upon high-velocity impact, allowing Slick to survive superhuman blows.

• Silhouette Profile: Adds approximately 1.5–2 inches of bulk to the torso and shoulders. Combined with height-boosting boots, it transforms Slick’s civilian silhouette (Mic) into a larger, broad-shouldered powerhouse.

II. THE WING: INVERNESS FLUIDIC COWL

A tiered, high-performance garment that serves as both a "cloak" and a variable-geometry flight surface.

• Design: Based on the Victorian Inverness Cape, featuring a sleeveless cowl over a full-length cape.

• Fluidic Thrust Vectoring: The cowl acts as a flexible nozzle. By adjusting the tension of "smart" piezoelectric threads in the fabric, Slick shapes the exhaust flow of the engine to bank, dive, or hover without traditional rudders.

• Stability Hem: The lower cape contains magnetic stiffening ribs to provide directional stability and prevents the suit from tumbling during high-G maneuvers.

III. THE DRIVE: MHD PLASMA ACCELERATOR

A non-combustion jetpack integrated into the spinal rig.

• Mechanic: Magnetohydrodynamics (MHD). Atmospheric air is ionized into plasma and accelerated using high-intensity magnetic fields rather than moving turbine blades.

• Flight Profile: Stealth-focused. Produces a high-frequency ultrasonic hum and a violet corona discharge (glow) at the exhaust vents.

• Performance: Capable of precision hovering and rapid "snapping" acceleration. Top speeds are limited to ~60mph to maintain "laminar flow" over the cape surface.

IV. THE POWER: MOLECULAR SPRING & FUEL CELL

Dual-mode energy storage for extended "Savant-level" mission times.

• Molecular Torsion Springs: High-density mechanical energy stored in carbon-nanotube ropes. Provides the massive "burst" power required for takeoff and combat maneuvers.

• Hydrogen Fuel Cell: A solid-oxide honeycomb integrated into the exoskeleton for "Cruise Mode," extending flight time to approximately 60 minutes.

• RF Harvesting: A "trickle-charge" system that draws ambient energy from city infrastructure (cell towers, power lines) to extend battery life in urban environments.

V. THE FAILSAFE: QUICK-RELEASE & PURGE

The "Savant’s" two-stage exit strategy.

• Quick-Release (Soft Exit): Uses magnetic bayonet mounts to decouple the cowl, gauntlets, and engine for orderly maintenance or civilian transition.

• The Purge (Hard Exit): An emergency "Scorched Earth" protocol.

• Mechanical: Explosive bolts jettison the suit components instantly.

• Thermite-Trace: Internal nano-thermite ignites, melting superconductors, silicon chips, and proprietary alloys into unidentifiable slag.

• Logic Bomb: Executes a code-wipe and structural shattering of crystalline memory before thermal destruction.
