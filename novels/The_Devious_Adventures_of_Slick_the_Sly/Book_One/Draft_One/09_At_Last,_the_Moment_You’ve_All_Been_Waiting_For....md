I had the cutest girls, the coolest friends. I wasn’t happy though. I did those things ‘cause my dad wanted me to. I thought that if everyone saw me like that, then I’d start to feel good about myself. I was a bit of a jerk, in some ways. I don’t like like to think about it much, because those were rough times.”

Slick pondered this for a moment before he replied. “Still, the models,” was all he said.

Greg gave a good hearted laugh. “The models are nice, sure, but you know why I go through women so quickly?” “Buffet logic?” Slick asked.

“What?” Greg laughed.

“Try a bit of everything so you get all the best stuff.” It wasn’t the most eloquent Slick had ever been, but it got his point across well enough.

Greg seemed amused by this. “Sort of, but not really. See, none of these girls make me happy to be with them. They’re just,” Greg groped for words for a moment, “empty. Their minds are all on the wrong things, and while they’re fun for a moment, they make me feel empty too.

That make sense?”

“I suppose so,” Slick replied, but though he understood the idea well enough, he wasn’t sure he could relate to what Greg said. At the end of the day, Greg still seemed to have a charmed life compared to Slick’s own. The grass always was greener though, or so Slick heard.

“You don’t sound convinced.” Greg smiled. “That’s okay. We all have our stuff, right? Just remember though, at the end of the day I’m just a guy, having as much trouble as you finding the same exact thing.”

What Greg said made a great deal of sense, and Slick sat back to ponder this new

insight.

“But hey man, don’t let her get to you. If she’s worth it, if she deserves you, she’ll see it. You’ll find a way to make it work.”

“And is she isn’t?” Slick was afraid he knew the answer.

Greg shrugged. “Move on. You’ll find someone, Mik. You deserve it.”

Something bothered Slick, and he had to ask Greg about it. “Greg, why are you doing

this?”

“Doing what?” he asked with genuine confusion.

“Talking to me. Helping me. We don’t know each other. We’re just neighbors, and we don’t even talk that often.”

Greg was careful with his response. “Well, you're right. I don’t know you that well, but you’ve been a good neighbor. I just,” Greg searched for words for a moment, “I think you’re a

good guy. A little odd sometimes, but your heart’s in the right place” If he only knew the truth, what would he say. Still, it was a touching thing to hear.

“I see,” Slick said, though nothing else came.

“Just remember, you deserve to be happy. Go do something that brings you joy. Get your mind off things.” Slick appreciated the advice, and already his mind moved on to other, more enjoyable topics. Greg was right. Someday, he’d get his next shot at Fiona. He’d meet her someday. She was worth the wait.

“You’re right, Greg. Why am I sitting out here? I’ve things to do.”

“That’s the spirit, Mik. I’m gonna head over to my place. You take care.” Greg waved and slipped off the swing. Slick jumped up and dashed into his house with a quick wave to his neighbor.

Spirits revitalized, Slick dashed to his lair and got to work. He spent a good bit of time working on the Delage. When he was done for the day, the car was pretty close to done. He still needed to order a few major parts, but they wouldn’t be too big of a hassle to install once he got them. The majority of the disassembly was done, now he just had to get everything installed. Slick went upstairs to his desk and ordered the parts he needed. Once that was taken care of, he was free to work on his specialized jetpack project. The systems still needed some tweaking, and the cosmetics needed a lot of work. Throughout the afternoon and into the evening, Slick made incredible progress towards his prototype.

He lamented the lost time yesterday. How much further could he have been if he’d only buckled down and gotten to work after his failed plan? No good would come of such thinking, so he kept on working until he could no longer keep his eyes open. By that point, he had a decent second version that even looked somewhat aesthetically pleasing. He hadn’t tested it yet, but perhaps he’d take care of that in morning. Actually, he quite looked forward to that. He’d never worked on something like this before, and the thrill and the danger of it all made him giddy. The excitement gave him such a rush that he stayed up an extra two hours to make tweaks and modifications to his current design. As a massive yawn overtook him, he looked on with pride at the jetpack set on its stand near the trophy room. Tomorrow would be a great day indeed.
